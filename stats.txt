Influencer Semantic Similarity Analysis: Data Stats & RAGs
========================================================

Total Data Points:
------------------
- Organic posts: ~5900 (organic_data.csv)
- Training posts: ~6100 (training_data.csv)
- Top influencer pairs analyzed: 10 (top5_influencers.csv, similarity_stats.csv)
- Sponsored posts per influencer (sample): 10 (top5_influencers.csv)

RAGs (Red-Amber-Green) Design:
------------------------------
- RAGs are used to visually represent similarity scores and improvement ratios for each influencer.
- Green: AI-modified sponsored content is significantly closer to organic tone (lower PN distance, higher similarity).
- Amber: Moderate improvement, but not statistically significant.
- Red: No improvement or original sponsored content is closer to organic tone.
- Thresholds are based on mean PN distance and t-test p-value (<0.05 is significant).

Ratios for Different Influencers (Sample):
------------------------------------------
- Mean Original Distance: 0.4746
- Mean Modified Distance: 0.4396
- Improvement Ratio: (0.4746 - 0.4396) / 0.4746 ≈ 7.4%
- t-statistic: 11.29
- p-value: 0.0000 (statistically significant)

Example Influencer (soheatherblog):
- Sponsored Count: 3
- Avg Original Similarity: 0.6099
- Avg Modified Similarity: 0.6127
- Improvement: 0.0027

General Workflow:
-----------------
- For each influencer, original and AI-modified sponsored posts are compared to organic posts using sentence embeddings.
- PN (Post-Neighbourhood) distance is calculated for each sponsored post to its 5 nearest organic posts.
- Paired t-test is performed to assess statistical significance of improvement.
- RAGs are assigned based on improvement and significance.

See similarity_stats.csv and final_comprehensive_report.csv for full influencer breakdown.
